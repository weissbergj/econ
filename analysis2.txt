While the analysis uses rigorous methods, we should be cautious about claiming pure causality. Here's why:
Research Design Strengths:
Uses difference-in-differences (DiD) and triple-difference designs
Includes parallel trends tests
Employs placebo tests
Controls for time and entity fixed effects
Potential Threats to Causality:
Significant
Selection Issues:
Industries may self-select into digital transformation
Large establishments might choose digitalization based on unobserved factors
Cannot fully rule out reverse causality (higher wages enabling digital investment)
Omitted Variables:
Other concurrent policy changes
Industry-specific trends
Local economic conditions
Labor market dynamics
The analysis suggests a strong relationship between digitalization, establishment size, and wages, but we should interpret it as "suggestive evidence" rather than definitively causal. The pre-trends and potential selection issues make it difficult to claim pure causality.
